        it took me 5 years (i started this in 2019 at my english teacher's recommendation) to work through the narcoleptic perverseness of what is my first murakami novel—but once the action picked up, i was able to blaze through the remaining 50% in the past week. the first half lulls you with its classic murakami-esque melancholy, then snaps to total mystery without warning. the end will leave you with an existential emptiness that you know won't be resolved. the only thing written in this book that's tethered to any sense of reality is the narrator's crude projected fantasies of unrequited sexual desire that probably take up a third of this book if you put them all together. but also you could take this review and stitch this to any one of murakami's books so if you're looking for anything of substance here im sorry
