<h4>451</h4>
<p>death speaks to me in threes and nines<br>
i check the clock as if a minute could tell me anything<br><br>
tell me,<br>
what must the body think after a day's long slumber,<br>
when it sees itself sewn up after being under the knife<br>
i hope i die on an even number<br>
i hope my body thinks it had healed itself overnight<br>
</p>

<h4>heaven is in the medicine cabinet</h4>
<p>i peel the fibers off each tangerine slice<br>
i don't like the taste of dirt and drought<br>
scrubbing my skin raw in the shower<br>
hoping a new body will no longer repent<br><br>
i watch the water rise to the surface<br>
as the drain struggles to swallow it down<br>
it sputters and cries, engorged with the filth of yesterday's sins<br>
but the purge will prevail, and the waste kept for another day<br><br>

god speaks through sonic soothsaying laughter,<br>
"you will be clean of it soon. you will be clean of it soon."<br>
but there was no blood to rinse off that bathroom faucet,<br>
nor was there a body that could ever be cleansed holy</p>
</li><br>
<li>
<h4>cerritos medical center</h4>
<p>there are medical encyclopedias - erudite chant, never a hymn<br>
never like the way i hear you sigh through the screen<br>
when i tell you i am carrying your sorrow again<br>
i tell the practitioner, "father has thick blood, mother has sick nerves"<br>
and she says, "your heart is not theirs"</p>
